About The product

Reading execution against expectation.

Conventional liquidity measures rely on bid-ask spreads, implied volatility, and dealer commentary — signals of what markets are pricing. Divergence reads what they are executing, and treats the gap between the two as the most informative signal on a session.

The framing borrows from the Tradeweb Liquidity Cost Index: liquidity measured in basis points actually traded, not implied. The asymmetry insight — that execution stressed with implied calm is the historically dangerous regime, not the inverse — is the central thesis.

Built on FastAPI, PostgreSQL with TimescaleDB and pgvector, and Claude Opus 4.7 for narrative authorship. Source available under MIT license.
